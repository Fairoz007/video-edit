# DocuForge — Cinematic documentary script template
# 1. Change TOPIC below to your subject.
# 2. Edit narration and visuals in each [section] block.
# 3. Save as .txt and upload in the app (Upload script tab).
# Duration hints are optional; video length follows your narration (spoken at ~150 WPM by default).

TOPIC: YOUR TOPIC HERE — e.g. The 2026 FIFA World Cup

[opening]
Scene Heading: Before the Storm
Title: Cold Open
Duration: 15
Visual Direction: Slow aerial drift over a vast stadium at blue hour; lens flare on floodlights; shallow depth of field.
B-Roll: aerial stadium night lights | empty football pitch fog dawn
Audio: Low drone pad; distant crowd murmur; single piano note.
Transition: Fade in from black; two seconds of silence before the first word.

Narration:
Some stories do not announce themselves.
They arrive like weather — quiet at first, then impossible to ignore.
This is the story of YOUR TOPIC HERE.
And it begins long before anyone thought to name it.

[introduction]
Scene Heading: The Central Question
Title: Why It Matters
Duration: 18
Visual Direction: Handheld intimacy — faces in crowd, tickets, flags; natural light; shallow focus.
B-Roll: documentary crowd portrait | stadium entrance wide
Audio: Sparse strings; room tone; heartbeat-adjacent pulse very low.
Transition: Match cut from wide skyline to close human detail.

Narration:
To understand what is coming, you have to sit with a question that will not go away.
Why does this moment matter — not on a poster, but in the lives of millions who will never touch the pitch?
The answer is not in a single date on a calendar.
It is in everything that had to break, bend, or burn to get here.

[backstory]
Scene Heading: Roots in Shadow
Title: Backstory
Duration: 22
Visual Direction: Archival grain, slower camera; maps, old footage, newspaper headlines if relevant.
B-Roll: vintage archive footage | old photographs documentary
Audio: Muted percussion; optional tape hiss; narration forward in the mix.
Transition: Dissolve between eras; L-cut on a key line.

Narration:
The past does not stay past.
Every decision made in boardrooms and back rooms still pulls on the present like gravity.
What looked inevitable from a distance was, up close, a chain of fragile choices under pressure.
Historians argue the details; the feeling remains the same — something enormous was already in motion.

[rising_action]
Scene Heading: Pressure Builds
Title: Rising Action
Duration: 35
Visual Direction: Quicker cuts, handheld energy, contrast rising; crowds, travel, construction, preparation.
B-Roll: timelapse city lights | athletes training silhouette
Audio: Rhythm enters; tension strings; crowd swell on cuts.
Transition: Smash cuts on impact words; cross-dissolve for geography jumps.

Narration:
Then the ground shifted.
What had been background noise became the main event — expansion, conflict, ambition, fear.
Critics and believers fought in public while planners moved pieces on a board the world could not fully see.
Momentum gathered, and with it stakes no one could pretend were small.
Every headline was a drumbeat; every delay, a held breath.

[revelation]
Scene Heading: The Unveiling
Title: Revelation
Duration: 25
Visual Direction: Stillness after motion; hold on a face or a decisive object; light changes on a key reveal.
B-Roll: dramatic window light portrait | document close-up hands
Audio: Music drops to near-silence; one sustained note.
Transition: Slow push-in; hold two beats after the line lands.

Narration:
And then — the detail that changes everything.
Not louder. Clearer.
The format, the host, the rule, the number — whatever your story's hinge is — stops being rumor and becomes fact.
In that instant, the future is no longer abstract.
It has a address, a date, and a price.

[climax]
Scene Heading: Breaking Point
Title: Climax
Duration: 32
Visual Direction: Peak contrast; full-frame action or emotional close-up; sound design carries weight.
B-Roll: dramatic stadium lights storm | celebration or protest crowd cinematic
Audio: Full score swell; impact SFX used sparingly; narration rides on top.
Transition: Optional white flash; return to image on the downbeat.

Narration:
This was the moment the idea became a force — felt in real rooms, real streets, real lives.
Not everyone celebrated; not everyone agreed.
But everyone understood: the world had chosen a direction, and there was no quiet way back.
The roar — or the silence — said what words could not.

[conclusion]
Scene Heading: Aftermath
Title: Conclusion
Duration: 18
Visual Direction: Softer light, wider frames, breathing room; dawn, empty corridors, quiet streets.
B-Roll: sunrise calm landscape | empty stadium seats morning
Audio: Warm strings resolve; ambience returns; music fades under narration.
Transition: Gentle dissolves; longer holds.

Narration:
When the noise faded, what remained was not a headline — but a changed landscape.
Legacies are not built in ninety minutes on a field; they are built in what people carry home afterward.
The story of YOUR TOPIC HERE is still being written in every choice that follows.

[ending]
Scene Heading: Last Light
Title: Ending
Duration: 15
Visual Direction: Single powerful image; hold; fade toward black.
B-Roll: cinematic sunset silhouette | night city bokeh lights
Audio: Music resolves to silence; one final ambient texture.
Transition: Fade to black; end on held image.

Narration:
Stories like this do not end.
They echo — in what we choose to remember, and what we dare to do next.
If this chapter taught us anything, it is that the future is still unwritten.
The only question left is who is willing to meet it.
